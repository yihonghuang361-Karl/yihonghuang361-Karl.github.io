I am a master's student in Party Affairs Management at East China Normal University. My work focuses on digital economy and digital governance, AI policy and firm resilience, platform economy and consumer behavior, higher education management, admissions operations, and innovation management.

I combine quantitative empirical methods with practical administrative experience in higher education. My current research uses firm-level data and policy evaluation designs to study how artificial intelligence policy affects organizational resilience and innovation-related outcomes in China.

# Working Papers

**The Impact of Artificial Intelligence Policy on Firm Resilience: Empirical Evidence from China**

First author; working paper, manuscript completed
- Built a firm-level empirical framework to estimate the impact of China's AI pilot zone policy on A-share listed firms from 2014 to 2023.
- Applied difference-in-differences models and robustness checks, including event-study diagnostics, parallel trend tests, IPTW/PSM-related specifications, SDID checks, threshold models, and heterogeneous effect analyses.
- Prepared Stata scripts, diagnostic tables, model outputs, and visualizations for reproducible empirical analysis.

**A Statistical Test of the Mutual Promotion Relationship between China's Political Development and Economic Development**

First author; manuscript under review at *Economic Research-Ekonomska Istrazivanja*
- Constructed an index system for China's political and economic development using the entropy weight method.
- Examined the long-term coupling and coordination relationship between political and economic development since 1978.

**Factors Influencing Purchase Intentions in Knowledge Live Streaming: A Family Life Cycle Perspective**

Bachelor's thesis; ranked 1st in the School of Management
- Developed a VAM-based consumer behavior framework to study purchase intention in knowledge live-streaming contexts.
- Applied SEM, CFA, and mediation analysis to examine the role of perceived value.

**Consumption Intention for Solo Dining Restaurants in Chengdu: A Singles Economy Perspective**

First author; competition research report
- Designed an eight-variable VAM-based framework and collected 1,160 valid survey responses through three-stage PPS sampling.
- Applied SEM, CFA, regression analysis, k-modes clustering, and neural-network methods to analyze mechanisms shaping consumption intention.
